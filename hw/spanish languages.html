Spanish languages

Spanish sometimes called Castilian, is the only official language of the entire country
and is spoken habitually and as a native language among a vast majority of the Spanish population.
It is also co-official with other languages in Catalonia, Balearic Islands, Valencia, Galicia, Basque Country and the Basque zone of Navarre.
In all of the bilingual autonomous communities, with the exception of Galicia,
Spanish is actually the native language of the majority of the population,
although this is due in large part to the internal migration processes that occurred in Spain from the middle of the 20th century.
